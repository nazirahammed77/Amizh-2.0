Birth of AMIZH Birth&Yoga

Giving birth doesn’t need to be scary like it is perceived? With a few practical things taken into account, you can have a happy and healthy pregnancy and birth. Years may pass, but one thing that stays evergreen in our memory is our pregnancy and birthing story. Through AMIZH Birth&Yoga, I look forward to helping and teaching Indian pregnant women living anywhere around the world to give them a birthing journey that they will cherish forever.

My Sweden Story

Coming from a humble family, my parents firmly believed in the power of education. Being the first graduate in the family and the first person to travel abroad to pursue my postgraduation and Doctorate dreams. I pursued my passion for Medicine, which had captivated me since childhood. During my time in Sweden, I worked in various medical science research facilities, conducted extensive doctoral research, and delved deep into the realms of human science and cutting-edge medical research technologies. My career took a beautiful turn when I confirmed my pregnancy & became a mother in September 2018 (the entire 40week journey happened in Sweden including giving birth) to our beautiful baby Boy-Aram Amizhthan.

My Sweden Story

The experience of giving birth to my son was incredibly beautiful and life-changing. We gave him a unique Tamil name as we wish him to remember in every moment of life that he is a unique and beautiful person as his name is. Even years later, the memories of my pregnancy journey and the joy of bringing new life into the world remain vivid and beautiful in my mind. This profound experience made me realize the importance of being prepared physically and emotionally to go through the birth journey in order to make it a mindful and happy experience.

My own pregnancy journey was far from easy—it was filled with extreme discomforts, especially during the first and third trimesters and had to go through a 48hr of labor before giving birth vaginally. I must admit that self-taught pregnancy education and yoga did help me go through those tough discomforts with ease and NO complications. Additionally, the exceptional care and support provided by the midwives in Sweden with not just labor but in breastfeeding assistance played a crucial role in ensuring me stepping in to motherhood with confidence.

Around the same time, I heard numerous birth stories from relatives and friends, including my own sister and sister-in-law, and realized that not all women happen to have a positive childbirth experience that they deserve. Many described their pregnancy journeys as filled with worries and fears, with unanswered questions and a lack of reliable sources for guidance. It became clear to me that there are so many myths and fears surrounding getting pregnant and giving birth that not all pregnant mothers neither have the confidence or experience the happiness they deserve.
